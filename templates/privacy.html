4. Data Retention

  • Account data — retained until you delete your account.
  • Authenticated scan results — retained with your account until deletion.
  • Guest scan results — automatically deleted after 90 days.
  • Gmail connection — OAuth tokens are kept (encrypted) until you disconnect Gmail or delete your account. Disconnecting also revokes the token at Google.
  • Analytics data — aggregated; no personal identifiers retained by us. Subject to Vercel's retention policy.

6. Cookies and Advertising

We set no non-essential cookies by default. We use Google Consent Mode, which keeps advertising and analytics signals set to "denied" until you choose Accept on the cookie banner.

  • Analytics. If you click Accept, Vercel Analytics may set cookies prefixed va- to count page views. No personal data is stored in these cookies.
  • Advertising. Content pages display ads from Google AdSense. Until you accept, ads are shown in non-personalised mode. If you click Accept, Google and its partners may set cookies to personalise ads and measure their performance. Ads are never shown on sign-in, dashboard, scan-result or error pages.

You can withdraw consent at any time by clicking Reject on the banner or by clearing this site's data in your browser. Your choice is stored in localStorage under the key sb_consent. You can also manage Google's personalised advertising at adssettings.google.com.

7. Third-party Services

  • Vercel — our hosting and infrastructure provider, and the provider of Vercel Analytics (if consented). See Vercel's Privacy Policy.
  • Google AdSense — serves the ads on our content pages. With your consent, Google and its partners may use cookies and similar technologies to personalise ads and measure performance; without consent, ads are non-personalised. See how Google uses information from sites that use its services and Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Threat intelligence APIs (URLhaus, ThreatFox, MalwareBazaar) — queried when you use the Threat Intel scanner. Only the URL, domain, IP, or hash you submit is sent. No account or personal data is shared.
  • Google (Gmail API) — used only if you connect Gmail in the Newsletter Manager. We request read-only access (gmail.readonly) and read only message headers to identify newsletters; this involves a transfer of data to Google (a US provider, covered by the EU–US Data Privacy Framework). See Google's Privacy Policy.

8. Gmail Newsletter Manager

This optional feature lets you connect your Gmail account to find and unsubscribe from newsletters. We want to be explicit about how it works:

  • Read-only, headers only. We request the minimal gmail.readonly scope and read only the From, Date, List-Unsubscribe and List-Unsubscribe-Post headers. We never read, store, or transmit the body or content of any email.
  • What we store. Only your OAuth tokens (encrypted at rest) and your Gmail address. The list of newsletters is generated on demand and shown to you — it is never saved.
  • Withdraw at any time. Click Disconnect in the Newsletter Manager to delete the stored tokens and revoke our access at Google. Deleting your account does the same automatically.
  • Security Buddy's use of information from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the service evolves. Material changes will be noted in the GitHub repository. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
